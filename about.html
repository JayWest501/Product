The story

How I got here
and why it matters.

Leaving the Air Force was harder than I expected. Not the work part – the people part. Seven years in Security Forces meant being surrounded by people from every background imaginable and learning how to work with all of them under pressure. When that ended, the transition to corporate life was quieter than I was ready for. More isolated. I knew how to execute, but I didn't yet know where I fit.

Implementation PM work changed that. It gave me back what I missed most – real problems, real people, and the kind of collaboration where your ability to read a room and build trust actually determines the outcome. Every client is different. Every implementation has a moment where the plan meets reality and you have to think fast.

Three years of that work put me in a specific seat: the person who sees what the product promised and what the client actually got. I watched well-intentioned features create months of cleanup. I watched small, well-scoped fixes save teams weeks of work, every week.

That's the perspective I'm bringing to product. I've already been on the receiving end of bad product decisions. I know what they cost. That's not something most APM candidates can say.

Outside of work I'm in the middle of my first running journey – several 5Ks and 10Ks in, with a half marathon on the calendar. The goal keeps moving forward. That feels right.
